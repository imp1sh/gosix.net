Services:

  • Phone
  • Broadband Internet Connectivity for Home & SMB - Brand: NET Vírtua
  • “Dedicated” links (Internet, IP Transit or Private WAN) for enterprise - Brand: Embratel
  • Other “value-added services” such as Anti-virus, tech support, home surveillance, music & books subscriptions, pet care etc.

Technologies:

  • Coaxial Cable (DOCSIS)
  • Mobile Wireless (5G, LTE, HSPA+, EDGE, GSM)
  • Licensed PtP Radio (Enterprise only)
  • Satellite (Enterprise only)
  • Fiber Optics

IPv6 Status:

  • Website main page is reachable over IPv6 via CDN, can't say about everything else (didn't test).
  • Home & SMB: Dual-stack enabled (shared address a.k.a. CGNAT on jurassic stack) via autoconfiguration (DHCP).
  • Mobile Wireless: Dual-stack available (varies by region, shared address a.k.a. CGNAT on jurassic stack), carrier-provided APN profile does not specify what protocol to use but their documentation states that users should manually set to Jurassic-only (IPv4).

Drawbacks:

  • Home & SMB: They offer /128 address for WAN + /64 prefix delegation. Their CPEs have some IPv6 settings available but it lacks things such as firewall rules, ULA address prefix, LAN prefix size etc.. LAN is set to DHCPv6-only by default - SLAAC is available but you can't enable both - and, in a considerable amount of cases, IPv6 is purposely disabled by their technicians for whatever reason. Switching to a user provided CPE is not restricted ("Bridge Mode" is available) and won't affect other services operation.
  • Enterprise: Allegedly available in every region but -1000000% interest in providing these to customers (not without charging extra for it) + most customers DGAF about it, anyways… but you can request stupid big blocks of jurassic addresses at anytime, no problem.
  • They are present in most brazilian IXPs (Internet Exchange Points) however they deny mutual peering agreements and peering with other ASes is ONLY by subscribing to their own links, unless someone agrees to peer with them in Europe or USA.
  • No Looking Glass available.
